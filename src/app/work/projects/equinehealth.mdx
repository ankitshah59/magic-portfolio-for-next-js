# Wearable Smart Textile Band for Continuous Equine Health Monitoring

**Purdue University** ‚Ä¢ *Biosensors and Bioelectronics, 2026*

## Overview

This study presents a **non-invasive, wireless smart textile band** designed specifically for continuous health monitoring in horses. The device tracks respiratory activity, cardiac function, and movement in real-time without requiring hair removal‚Äîaddressing a critical gap in equine healthcare where nearly **80% of racehorses suffer from asthma** but lack practical long-term monitoring solutions.

üí° **Key Innovation**: First field-deployable, hair-coat-tolerant wearable system for equines that enables continuous physiological monitoring during natural behavior, validated against clinical gold standards.

---

## The Problem

### Equine Health Crisis

**Respiratory Diseases:**
- **80% of racehorses** affected by mild to moderate asthma
- **14% of adult horses** in general population have asthma
- Diminished athletic performance
- Increased veterinary costs
- Career-ending health complications

**Cardiovascular Issues:**
- Less prevalent but performance-impairing
- Exercise-induced cardiac arrhythmias
- Closely linked to respiratory function

### Current Monitoring Limitations

**Traditional Methods:**
- Require restraining the animal (induces stress)
- Periodic assessments miss transient abnormalities
- Subjective observations (inconsistent)
- Auscultation, ECG, endoscopy (invasive)
- Delayed diagnoses

**Existing Wearables:**
- Require shaving hair (impractical)
- Lack wireless functionality
- Not portable or robust
- Short-term clinical use only
- Motion artifacts compromise signals

**Critical Need:** Non-invasive, wireless, continuous monitoring for early detection and timely intervention in equines.

## System Design

### Smart Textile Band Architecture

**Core Components:**

- **MEMS Microphone**: Respiratory sounds, Ultra-low noise (ICS-40720, TDK)
- **3-axis Accelerometer**: Respiration + heartbeat vibrations, BMI 160 (Bosch)
- **3-axis Gyroscope**: Movement tracking, Integrated with accelerometer
- **Temperature Sensor**: Body temperature, Real-time monitoring
- **BLE SoC**: Wireless data transmission, nRF52840 (Nordic)
- **Battery**: Power supply, 150 mAh, 15+ hours operation

**Key Innovation:** Uses **mechano-acoustic sensing** via accelerometer to capture both respiration and heart rate from local vibrations, without interference from global body movements.

### Mechanical Design Features

**Flexible PCB Structure:**
- Three-layer stacked, three-island design
- 32 active and passive components
- Serpentine interconnectors for flexibility
- Compatible textile band between layers 1 and 2

**Encapsulation:**
- Soft, waterproof elastomer (Ecoflex 00-35)
- Protects electronics from sweat and environmental factors
- Maintains flexibility and comfort

**Adjustability:**
- Size-adjustable textile band
- Accommodates various equine body types
- Secure fit without restricting movement
- Can stretch and twist freely

### Mechanical Validation

**Finite Element Analysis:**
- Maximum principal strain less than 1% under 10% stretching + twisting
- Elastic modulus: 913 kPa (device-embedded textile) vs 689 kPa (bare textile)
- Withstands up to 140% strain before breaking

**Environmental Durability:**
- Repeated saline solution exposure (simulates sweat)
- Maintained full functionality under continuous flow
- No signal degradation during extended use

## Technical Performance

### Benchtop Validation

**Vibration Response Testing:**

- **0.13 Hz** (Low respiratory rate): 39.36 dB SNR
- **0.27 Hz** (High respiratory rate): 36.32 dB SNR
- **0.47 Hz** (Low heart rate): 35.73 dB SNR
- **0.67 Hz** (High heart rate): 39.55 dB SNR

**Key Finding:** System captures clear signals across entire physiological frequency range for equine respiration (0.13-0.27 Hz) and cardiac activity (0.47-0.67 Hz).

### Signal Processing Methodology

**Continuous Wavelet Transform:**
- Analytic Morlet wavelet for time-frequency analysis
- **Respiratory rate:** Frequencies below 0.5 Hz
- **Heart rate:** Frequencies 5-15 Hz
- 20-second non-overlapping windows for localized analysis

**Peak Detection:**
- Valley identification (breath cycles, heartbeats)
- Dynamic threshold to minimize false detections
- Conversion to BPM: 60 divided by Average Interval (seconds)

**SNR Calculation:**
- Power spectral density estimation
- SNR in dB equals 10 times log‚ÇÅ‚ÇÄ of signal power divided by noise power
- Chest Z-axis acceleration for HR: 14.34 dB (sufficient for reliable detection)

## In Vivo Validation Results

### Resting State Monitoring (30 Minutes)

**Healthy Horse (16-year-old Standardbred):**

**Respiratory Rate:**
- Clinical equipment: 7.11 BPM
- Flow sensor: 7.14 BPM
- Microphone: 7.10 BPM
- **Smart textile band (Z-axis): 7.04 BPM**

**Heart Rate:**
- ECG (gold standard): 34.64 BPM
- **Smart textile band (Z-axis): 34.54 BPM**

**Bland-Altman Analysis:**
- RR mean difference: 0.06 BPM (deviation: 0.53 BPM)
- HR mean difference: 0.10 BPM (deviation: 1.65 BPM)

**Excellent agreement with clinical gold standards**

---

### Treadmill Exercise Testing

**Exercise Protocol:**
- Walking: 1.5 m/s for 15 min
- Trotting: 4.5 m/s for 30 min
- Walking: 1.5 m/s for 6 min

**Respiratory Rate Results:**

- **Walking (initial)**: Clinical 60.47 BPM, Flow 60.11 BPM, Textile 62.34 BPM
- **Trotting**: Clinical 110.96 BPM, Flow 110.06 BPM, Textile 110.89 BPM
- **Walking (recovery)**: Clinical 66.91 BPM, Flow 68.57 BPM, Textile 71.02 BPM

**Heart Rate Results:**

- **Walking (initial)**: ECG 59.09 BPM, Textile 58.94 BPM
- **Trotting**: ECG 102.82 BPM, Textile 104.16 BPM
- **Walking (recovery)**: ECG 72.73 BPM, Textile 75.08 BPM

**Bland-Altman During Exercise:**
- RR mean difference: 1.06 BPM (deviation: 4.39 BPM)
- HR mean difference: 0.90 BPM (deviation: 4.49 BPM)

**Signal Quality:**
- Chest ECG SNR: 15.41 dB
- Chest Z-axis acceleration SNR: 14.51 dB

**Maintained accuracy during dynamic movement**

### Asthma Detection and Treatment Monitoring

**Asthmatic Horse (18-year-old Tennessee Walker):**

**Before Bronchodilator Administration:**

**Respiratory Rate:**
- Clinical equipment: 15.24 BPM
- Flow sensor: 15.35 BPM
- Smart textile band: 14.98 BPM

**Heart Rate:**
- ECG: 43.51 BPM
- Smart textile band: 43.18 BPM

**After Bronchodilator (N-butylscopolammonium bromide, 0.3 mg/kg IV):**

**Respiratory Rate (decreased 4 BPM):**
- Clinical equipment: 11.10 BPM
- Flow sensor: 11.36 BPM
- Smart textile band: 11.36 BPM

**Heart Rate (increased 15 BPM):**
- ECG: 58.27 BPM
- Smart textile band: 58.30 BPM

**Asthma Severity Indicators:**

**Abdominal Movement Intensity:**
- **Jerk (Z-axis acceleration gradient):** Decreased 36%
- **Energy Expenditure (gyroscope RMS):** Decreased 44%
- **Transpulmonary pressure:** Decreased 71% (85.97 to 25.30 cmH‚ÇÇO)

**Clinical Pulmonary Function Changes:**
- Dynamic compliance (Cdyn): Increased
- Lung resistance (Rl): Decreased
- Peak flows (PIF/PEF): Improved
- Tidal volume, minute ventilation: Normalized

**Clinical Breakthrough:** First demonstration of wearable device detecting asthma severity and monitoring treatment response in real-time, non-invasively. The smart textile band successfully quantified the physiological changes characteristic of bronchodilator administration, matching clinical equipment accuracy.

## Key Advantages

### Hair-Coat Tolerance

**Problem Solved:**
- Traditional sensors require shaving (impractical, uncomfortable)
- Affects animal appearance and welfare

**Solution:**
- Mechano-acoustic sensing penetrates hair coat
- Accelerometer detects vibrations through fur
- No skin contact required for respiratory/cardiac monitoring

### Wireless and Portable

**Specifications:**
- Bluetooth Low Energy transmission
- 15+ hours battery life (150 mAh, 9.82 mA average current)
- Real-time data visualization
- No tethered connections restricting movement

### Multi-Parameter Monitoring

**Simultaneous Measurements:**
- Respiratory rate (breath-by-breath)
- Heart rate (beat-to-beat)
- Body temperature
- Movement patterns (accelerometer + gyroscope)
- Energy expenditure
- Activity classification (walking, trotting, resting)

### Clinical-Grade Accuracy

**Validation Against Gold Standards:**
- Ultrasonic phase-shift flowmeters (respiratory)
- ECG (cardiac)
- Esophageal balloon catheter (transpulmonary pressure)
- Pneumotachometer (pulmonary function)

**Bland-Altman Agreement:**
- Resting: within 0.5 BPM (RR), within 1.7 BPM (HR)
- Exercise: within 4.4 BPM (RR), within 4.5 BPM (HR)
- Asthma: within 0.7 BPM (RR), within 1.9 BPM (HR)

### Robust to Motion Artifacts

**Challenge:** Movement during exercise obscures physiological signals

**Solution:**
- CWT filtering isolates specific frequency bands
- Local vibration sensing (chest/abdomen) vs global movement
- Maintained greater than 14 dB SNR during trotting (4.5 m/s)

## Impact Statement

This work introduces the first **field-deployable, hair-coat-tolerant wearable system** for continuous equine health monitoring, validated against clinical gold standards across healthy and asthmatic horses. By enabling **non-invasive, real-time detection** of respiratory and cardiac abnormalities during natural behavior, the smart textile band addresses a critical unmet need in veterinary medicine.

The demonstrated ability to **quantify asthma severity and monitor treatment response** represents a significant advancement for the 80% of racehorses affected by this condition. Beyond equines, the principles established here‚Äîmechano-acoustic sensing through hair/fur, motion-artifact-resistant algorithms, and field-deployable design‚Äîhave **broad applicability to other large animals and human populations** where traditional monitoring is impractical.
